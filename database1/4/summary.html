Grand Area Article Summary and Highlights

The article has three parts. It begins with a discussion of the Council on Foreign Relations (wiki) as one key policy-discussion organization in the corporate-sponsored policy-planning network that is an essential link between the corporate community and government. The section pays special attention to the origins of the CFR’s War-Peace Studies, which gradually developed a strategic plan for a “Grand Area” economic region that provided the framework for postwar foreign and economic policy-making. The article then turns in the second and third sections to case studies of the origins of the IMF and the Vietnam War to document the way in which the Grand Area plan and the members of the War-Peace Study Groups that created it played the critical role in postwar planning.
