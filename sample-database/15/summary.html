- Galileo observed Jupiter in 1610, he saw giant orbs moving around it the same way they would if they were attached by springs. Sin wave motion, identical mathematically to motion of object on spring. Thinking of moons as attached by springs, would make exactly the same predictions as the gravity model when viewing moons from the side. Both pictures correctly describe observations. - Quantum phenomena can be modeled in three different ways that give same predictions:

1. Bohm Model. Particle being guided by spread out pilot wave. (particle is real)


2. Shrodinger Model. Spread out probability wave that collapses to a single point. (wave function is real)


3. Feynmann Model. Particle exploring all possible paths it could take and interfering with itself. (shut up and calculate)
